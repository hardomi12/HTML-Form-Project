Environment Survey

San Marcos Ecology Coalition is an alliance of citizens dedicated to educating others about the natural world and improving human interactions with the environment. As part of our mission, we conduct research on local attitudes towards and experiences with nature, covering topics such as encounters with plant life and animal life, littering/pollution, and time spent outdoors. By filling out this simple survey, you will help our coalition analyze local sentiments toward nature to inform future educational sources on deep ecology and further research.

Browse the rest of our website to learn more about our organization and how you can help.

This is a short, voluntary survey. If at any time you do not wish to complete the survey, you may exit the website. Please select the answers that best fit your honest opinions and life experience, making sure to address every question. No sensitive information will be taken for this survey.


1. How many hours a day do you spend outside?


2. Are you satisfied with the amount of time you currently spend outdoors?

3. What activity takes up the majority of your time outside?

4. Which of the following describes your inability to spend more time in nature?


5. Are you knowledgeable about the animal life local to your area?

6. Are you knowledgeable about the plant life local to your area?


7. Are you familiar with the concept of deep ecology?

8. Which of the following describes your view of the natural world?
